Kealakekua Bay Snorkel

Sun, Jun 21
Confirmed · $436.00
Small-Group Snorkel at Kealakekua Bay — Captain Cook
11:00 AM · 3 Adults + 1 Child
One of the finest snorkeling spots in all of Hawaii — a protected marine preserve with crystal-clear water, spinner dolphins, and abundant tropical fish. The Captain Cook Monument is visible across the bay. Small-group format means a more personal, less crowded experience.
🗺 78-7138 Kaleiopapa St, Kailua-Kona 🚗 From Hale Mar: ~2 hrs 30 min ⚠️ Leave Hale Mar by 8:15 AM
🌺 After the Snorkel — Captain Cook / South Kona Area
Nearby Option
Kona Coffee Living History Farm ⭐ 4.6
Right in Captain Cook — a living museum where staff personally share stories of Japanese tenant coffee farming families. You can feed the donkey, crack macadamia nuts, and pick coffee berries. Intimate and deeply personal. Only open Tue & Fri, 10 AM–2 PM. Worth planning around!
🗺 82-6199 Hawaiʻi Belt Rd, Captain Cook 🚗 2 min from snorkel meetup ⚠️ Tue & Fri only · 10 AM–2 PM
Nearby Option
Punaluʻu Black Sand Beach ⭐ 4.7
Hawaii's famous black volcanic sand beach — one of the most unique in the world. Endangered Hawaiian green sea turtles (honu) bask directly on the sand and are a common sight. Stunning contrast of jet-black sand, turquoise water, and green palms. A perfect afternoon detour heading back east.
🗺 Punaluʻu Beach, Naalehu, HI 🚗 From Captain Cook: ~1 hr south · Then to Hale Mar: ~1 hr 30 min 💰 Free

Kona Manta Ray Night Snorkel — FREE Re-ride if No Sightings
7:50 PM departure · 4 Adults
Snorkel alongside giant Pacific manta rays as they perform graceful barrel rolls feeding on plankton illuminated by underwater lights. These gentle giants can reach 12–16 feet across. An otherworldly, bucket-list experience — completely safe and unforgettable. Guaranteed mantas or you ride again for free.
🗺 78-7138 Kaleiopapa St, Kailua-Kona 🚗 From Hale Mar: ~2 hrs ⚠️ Evening snorkel — Leave Hale Mar by 3:30 PM to enjoy Kona first
💡 Make a full Kona day of it! Since the snorkel isn't until 7:50 PM, leave Hale Mar mid-morning (~10 AM) and spend the whole day in Kona — coffee farm tour, beach time, shopping on Aliʻi Drive, pre-snorkel dinner — then the night snorkel caps it perfectly. Drive home after (~9:30–10 PM arrival).
